Safety First

Warning Signs You Shouldn't Ignore

Electrical problems rarely fix themselves. Recognize these signs and protect your home before small issues become emergencies.

Flickering or Dimming Lights

Could indicate loose wiring, overloaded circuits, or a failing panel.

!

Burning Smell from Outlets

Immediate fire hazard. Turn off power and call immediately.

!

Warm or Discolored Outlets

Sign of dangerous overheating that can lead to electrical fires.

Frequent Breaker Trips

Your circuits are overloaded or there's a short somewhere.

!

Sparks When Plugging In

Internal arcing that can ignite nearby materials.

Buzzing Sounds from Panel

Loose connections or failing breakers need immediate attention.

Outlets Don't Hold Plugs

Worn outlets are a shock and fire risk, especially around children.

Two-Prong Outlets (No Ground)

Outdated outlets without ground protection increase shock risk.

Seeing any of these signs?

Don't wait. Electrical problems get worse—and more dangerous—over time.
